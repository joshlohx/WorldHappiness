Understanding Happiness Across Countries

Data Source: World Happiness Report 2023

A Global View

Taking a step back, we can see that Oceania, Europe, North America & Asia are the happiest on average. What these continents have in common is how developed they are (this correlation is explored later in the scatterplot!).

These scores are taken from the Gallup World Poll, which connects with over 99% of the globe! Participants are asked to rank their happiness on a scale of 1 to 10.

A Surprising Trend

A steady increase of the global happiness score is evident from 2017 to 2022. What is interesting about this is that this trend persisted throughout COVID-19.

A possible explanation for this seemingly irrational relationship is that during the pandemic and lockdown individuals were prompted to prioritise their well-being, mental health and relationships. Additionally, there was a shift of white-collar professionals to remote work.

A Strong Correlation

It is evident that GDP has a clear positive correlation with happiness scores. A strong GDP per capita generally leads to improved living standards, access to better healthcare, education and overall prosperity.

A stark outlier present is Afghanistan. Afghanistan has over 3.5x the global's average perception of corruption and is also one of the lowest GDP per capita countries.

A Clear Leader

For 2023, Finland was the happiest country in the world. This shouldn't come as much of a surprise though, as this is the 6th year in a row that Finland has earned the top spot!

This can likely be attributed to Finland's 1st ranked freedom to make life choices and 2nd ranked social support.
